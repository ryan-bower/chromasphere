Bernard said, “Most of planet’s atmosphere is too hot. We will have to enter the spot that is the coolest out of all atmosphere."

Lee looked at the map, “But we will land on the desert.”

Bernard answered, “No choices, better than die in burning or exploding.”

Lee and Bernard got off the rocket and he asked Bernard, “Where are we anyway?”

Bernard answered, “You asked a wrong koala, boy, but we won’t gain anyway if we waited. We need to find a village.”

Both of them could only see nothing, but miles and miles of desert surrounding them. They picked up what is left of water and foods before they were travelling through the desert.

For hours, they still couldn’t find anything in the middle of the desert. Lee was getting worried because their water was running out, as they lacked access to safe drinking water when you're traveling. He noticed that Bernard was becoming dehydrated; he wouldn’t stop grumbling some nonsense. Lee looked around once again and finally found a huge pile of rocks. He dragged Bernard, walking as fast as he can.

It was quite a trip before they finally arrived at the rocks. Bernard sighed, “Ahh, I am so glad that there is a shade. I feel like I am about to have a heatstroke. Stupid shitty fur. Making me sweating. Boy, I smell like Hippo piss.”

“Quiet! Did you hear that?” whispered Lee. The ground was little shaking. “What was that?”

Both of them climbed over the rocks and found a large scale battle between two tribes across the valley. They were just watching in awe.

Bernard whispered, “I think we better gotta our hairy asses of here.”

Lee nodded in agreement. However, before they came to realize, a group of men on the horses were already on their way to the rocks.

Bernard shouted, “RUN FOR YOUR LIFE!”

Lee grabbed Bernard, put him on his back and ran as fast as he could. Bernard looked back and could see the horses were getting closer and closer. He shouted, “We won’t make it!!”

Lee looked back in panic and suddenly, he slipped on the edge of a steep hill. He fell and rolled all way down. Bernard was flying into the air like bird as if it was shot. When he finally stopped rolling at the bottom of the hill, he immediately checked on the men who were chasing after them. They just stopped on the top of the hill and turned around back. Lee sighed in relief. However, the battle was practically a few feet away from the rocks where the boy was hiding. “I don’t know where we can go from this” thought Lee. He got startled when Bernard’s legs popped out of the sand. He ran and pulled him out of the sand. Bernard shaked his head to get the sand out of his big ears and saw the boy, but the shadow person was standing behind him.

Rather than destruction and bloody, it was a paradise everywhere. Those red tribes love war more than anything. They lived for it. It was a honor to die in combat. Their families won’t be sad if one of their members died in battle. Again, it was really a paradise because their economies were booming and everyone were practically living a luxury life. Lee was fairly good in history classes. He was well-aware that war was an awful thing and many unnecessary lives were lost. Yet, this culture was completely different and he still can’t believe it.

Also, Lee found it quite odd because the war was their daily activity and many lives were lost unfortunately, so, the population should be declining. Yet, the number of the population had been still the same for hundreds years. The number didn’t increase or decrease. It was just stable. The boy couldn’t stop being astround. If it were like that on Earth, it would be in disaster.

This war has been lasting for really a long time. It is raging for more than hundreds years. Perhaps more than that. All we know is those tribes are the causing of this damages.”

Lee was quieted for a while. He still didn’t understand their culture at all. He still believed the war is wrong. He couldn’t bear the thought of losing a family member to war.
